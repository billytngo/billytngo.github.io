ACM Format Guide

General Format for Any Citation

Author. Publication Year. Title. Title of container (self contained if book) [vol number], [issue number] ({Jan-Dec}. Year), [page number 1]-[page number 2]. Publisher, Location.

Citation with One Author

First name Middle Initials Last name

Thomas J. Berndt. 2002. Friendship quality and social development. Current Directions in Psychological Science 11, 1, 7-10.

Citation with Two Authors or More

List by first names middle initials and last names; commas separate author names, while the last author name is preceded again by and.

Michael H. Kernis, David P. Cornell, Chien R. Sun, Andrea Berry, and Thomas Harlow. 1993. There's more to self-esteem than whether it is high or low: The importance of stability of self-esteem. Journal of Personality and Social Psychology 65, 6, 1190-1204.

Examples

1. Cite a Book

David Kosiur. 2001. Understanding Policy-Based Networking (2nd. ed.). Wiley, New York, NY.

2. Cite a Paper in Journal

Patricia S. Abril and Robert Plant. 2007. The patent holder's dilemma: Buy, sell, or troll? Commun. ACM 50, 1 (Jan. 2007), 36-44.

3. Cite a Page on Website

Harry Thornburg. 2001. Introduction to Bayesian Statistics. (March 2001). Retrieved March 2, 2005 from http://ccrma.stanford.edu/~jos/bayes/bayes.html
